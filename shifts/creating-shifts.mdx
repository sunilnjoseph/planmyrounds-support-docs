There are three ways to create a shift in PlanMyRounds: from a **template**, from **scratch**, or by **bulk import**.

## Create from a template

The fastest way — recommended for any shift you run regularly.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the roster">
    Go to **Rosters → \[your roster\] → Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a template">
    From the right-hand panel, drag a template (e.g. _Day SHO 08:00–20:00_) onto a person/date cell.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The shift inherits the template's times, role, and breaks. Edit any field inline if needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create from scratch

Use this for one-off cover, ad-hoc shifts, or training days.

1. In the roster, click an empty cell or hit **\+ Add shift**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Start / end time** (crossing midnight is supported)
   - **Role** (e.g. SHO, Registrar, Consultant)
   - **Location** (ward, site, department)
   - **Break** (paid / unpaid)
   - **Notes** (visible to the assignee)
3. Click **Save**.

<Note>
  Shifts created from scratch are not linked to a template — future template edits won't propagate to them.
</Note>

## Create recurring shifts

For rotating patterns (e.g. _1 in 4 weekends_):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the shift editor">
    Click **\+ Add shift** and toggle **Recurring**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the pattern">
    Choose frequency (daily, weekly, custom n-week cycle) and the start/end of the recurrence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose handling for conflicts">
    Decide whether to **skip** dates that overlap leave/existing shifts, or **flag** them for review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    PlanMyRounds creates one shift per occurrence. You can edit or delete individual instances later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Bulk import

For moving an existing rota in from a spreadsheet:

1. Go to **Rosters → Import**.
2. Download the CSV template.
3. Fill in `date`, `start_time`, `end_time`, `role`, `assignee_email`, `location`, `template_code` (optional).
4. Upload — PlanMyRounds will validate rows and show a preview before committing.

<Warning>
  Imports respect leave and rest rules. Conflicting rows are listed for manual resolution; they are **not** silently dropped.
</Warning>

## Validation rules

When you save a shift, PlanMyRounds checks:

- The assignee has no overlapping shift on that date.
- The assignee has no approved leave covering the shift.
- Minimum rest between shifts (configured in **Organisation Settings**) is respected.
- The assignee holds the role required by the shift.

Failures appear inline and must be resolved or overridden (with reason) before saving.
