A roster lives in **draft** until you **publish** it. Only published shifts are visible to staff and counted in rotas, exports, and integrations.

## Publish a roster

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the roster">
    Go to **Rosters → \[your roster\]**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the pre-publish check">
    Click **Review** to see:

    - Unassigned shifts
    - Conflicts (rest, leave, role)
    - Under/over-contracted staff
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve or acknowledge">
    Fix what you can; acknowledge anything you're knowingly publishing with gaps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Publish">
    Choose the date range and whether to notify staff immediately or on a schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What staff receive

On publish, each affected user gets a single consolidated notification — not one per shift.

| Channel | When | Customisable? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **In-app** | Always | No |
| **Email** | On publish, swaps, cover, changes | Yes, per-user |
| **Push (mobile)** | On publish, day-before reminder | Yes, per-user |
| **SMS** | Optional, for urgent changes | Yes, org-level |

Users control their own channels under **Profile → Notifications**.

## Publishing changes after the fact

If you edit a published roster, the change is published immediately. Affected staff get a **change notification** showing exactly what changed:

- New shift added
- Shift removed
- Shift edited (with before/after)
- Reassignment

<Warning>
  Changes within the **lockout window** (default: 24h before the shift starts) require a reason and trigger an SMS to affected staff.
</Warning>

## Unpublishing

You cannot fully unpublish a roster, but you can:

- **Revert individual shifts** to draft — they disappear from staff views and the change is logged.
- **Cancel a shift** — leaves the slot empty but keeps the history.

## Notification settings

Org-level settings (in **Organisation Settings → Notifications**):

- Quiet hours (e.g. no push notifications 22:00–07:00)
- Default channels for new users
- Reminders (day-before, hour-before)
- Digest mode (one email per day vs. real-time)

## Best practices

<Tip>
  Publish on a predictable cadence (e.g. every Friday for 4 weeks ahead) — staff trust the rota more when it appears at a known time.
</Tip>

- Run **Review** before every publish — it surfaces issues you'd otherwise hear about from staff.
- Use the **lockout window** to prevent last-minute silent edits.
- Encourage staff to enable push notifications for swap/cover offers.
